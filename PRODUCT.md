Recap is a YouTube video summarizer for students. The product is a paste-a-link-get-a-study-summary tool. Speed and clarity are the headline; the experience must feel effortless and calm. The complete product description / single source of truth lives in `_doc/product-overview.md` and is authoritative.

## Name
Recap

## What it is
A study tool that takes a YouTube lecture link and returns a clean, student-focused summary within a couple of minutes. The summary captures: key points, important definitions/terms, and the concepts most likely to appear on a test. The input is a YouTube link only (NOT a file upload).

## The problem it solves
Lectures are long and dense; the value is buried in time. Re-watching a 60-90 minute recording to find the few minutes that matter is slow and discouraging. Recap turns one long lecture into a readable, study-ready summary fast.

## Who it's for
- Primary: students reviewing recorded lectures, cramming key points before exams.
- Secondary: educators / course creators who want quick summaries of their own recorded sessions to share or repurpose as study notes.

## Core day-one flow (the one brilliant thing)
1. User pastes a YouTube lecture link.
2. App pulls/transcribes the spoken content from the video.
3. App produces a structured, student-focused summary: key points, key terms/definitions, and concepts most likely to appear on a test.
4. User reads or copies the summary.

The verified operation is a lecture summarized — one YouTube lecture turned into a delivered summary in the deployed app.

## Scope
- In (day one): YouTube link input, transcription, AI-generated student-focused structured summary (key points, definitions, test-likely concepts), basic readable/copyable output, a clear in-progress/loading state while the summary is being produced (target: a couple of minutes).
- Out (day one): saving notes, flashcards, highlights, searchable library of past summaries, export/share, live/real-time capture, collaborative editing, multi-language guarantees.

## Positioning, brand & tone
- Positioning: a fast, no-friction "paste a link, get a study summary" tool for students. Speed and clarity are the headline.
- Tone: clear, focused, calm, study-friendly. Not gimmicky. Trustworthy and academic without being stuffy.

## Strategic principles
- Speed is the differentiator — the summary must arrive fast and feel effortless.
- Summaries must be useful, not a transcript dump: structure and signal over volume, framed for studying (what to remember, what's likely on the test).
- Keep the first run to one brilliant thing: paste a YouTube lecture link, get a good student-ready summary.

## Anti-references
- Not a transcript dump tool — the output must be distilled and structured, never a wall of raw text.
- Not a general AI chat interface — the home flow is single-purpose: one link field, one clear call to action.
- Not a cluttered productivity suite — day one is one focused job done well.
